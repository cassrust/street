Van Ness

Before the 1906 San Francisco earthquake, Van Ness Avenue was known as "the citys grandest boulevard, lined with Victorian mansions and impressive churches" (San Francisco Chronicle).[6] After the earthquake, the street was used as a firebreak by the US Army, dynamiting almost all buildings on its eastern side in an ultimately successful attempt to prevent the firestorm from spreading west to the entire city.[6]
